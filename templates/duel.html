PvP Minesweeper — Real-Time Competitive Play

PvP mode puts you into a live 1v1 Minesweeper match against a randomly matched opponent. Both players receive the same board, start simultaneously on first click, and race to clear as many tiles as possible before time runs out. Your score is compared against your opponent's in real time — the higher score wins the round.

Unlike Duel mode (which uses a private link to challenge a specific friend), PvP uses automatic matchmaking: click Standard or Quick and you'll be paired with another player waiting in the queue. No link to share, no waiting room to manage — just a match.

How Matchmaking Works

When you open the PvP lobby, you're assigned a player ID and placed in a matchmaking queue. As soon as another player enters the same queue, a game room is created automatically and both players are connected. The board is revealed and the clock starts on your first click.

Standard mode uses the full 9×9 Beginner board (10 mines). Quick mode (⚡) uses a smaller board for faster rounds — ideal when you want a faster pace or are warming up.

vs Bot is available from the tab bar above if no live opponent is available. The bot plays at Medium difficulty by default; harder difficulties are available from the bot lobby.

How Scoring Works

Your score is calculated in two parts:

  • 5 points per tile revealed. Every safe cell you uncover earns 5 points. Chord-clicking through multiple cells at once earns points for each cell revealed.
  • Time bonus: (300 − secondsFromStart) × percentRevealed, minimum 0. Finish quickly with a high percentage cleared and the time bonus can rival the tile score. The bonus decays to 0 after 300 seconds.

The player with the higher total score wins. If both players clear the board completely, the faster time is the tiebreaker. Hitting a mine doesn't end the game — it costs you no direct points, but the explosion animation briefly interrupts your board, and you lose the tiles you were about to chord through.

Rankings and Leaderboard

Competitive PvP tracks win/loss records and an ELO-style rating across all your matches. Your rating rises when you beat higher-rated opponents and falls when you lose to lower-rated ones. The leaderboard ranks players by rating.

View the full leaderboard at pvp/leaderboard and the ELO rankings at pvp/rankings. Your personal record appears on your profile page once you've played at least one rated match.

Strategy for Competitive PvP

  1. Open fast, chord faster. The time bonus rewards both speed and coverage. Start with a center click to maximize the opening cascade, then chord through all completed constraints immediately.
  2. Don't over-flag. Right-clicking takes time. In PvP, many top players skip flagging entirely and rely on memory, using the spare clicks on more tile reveals instead.
  3. Watch your opponent's score. If you're trailing, play more aggressively — take risky 50/50 guesses to catch up. If you're ahead, slow down and play safely to preserve your lead.
  4. Practice in solo first. PvP uses the same board mechanics as Beginner. If your solo Beginner time is above 60 seconds, practice there first — competitive PvP rewards consistent sub-30s board clearing.
  5. Quick mode for warm-ups. The smaller Quick board completes in 10–20 seconds. Use it to get in rhythm before playing rated Standard rounds.

Minesweeper 1v1 Duel — Challenge a Friend

Duel mode is private 1v1 Minesweeper: you create a room, get a shareable link, and send it to exactly one person. Both players receive the same board, start on their first click, and race to the higher score. No matchmaking queue, no random opponent — just you vs. someone you choose.

Duel is the right mode for challenges with friends, teammates, or anyone you want to beat specifically. The room link is single-use — once two players have joined, the game begins. There are no ELO ratings or persistent rankings in Duel mode; it's purely for the match itself.

How to Start a Duel

  1. Open /duel and click Create Room (or wait — a room is created automatically when the page loads).
  2. Copy the share link that appears in the status bar and send it to your opponent.
  3. Wait for them to join. Once both players are connected, click Start to begin.
  4. Both boards appear side by side. Your board is interactive; your opponent's is a live read-only view with a configurable delay.

The opponent board delay (visible on the badge above their board) is a fairness setting — it means you see your opponent's board a few seconds behind real time, so you can't simply copy their moves.

How Scoring Works

Both players are scored on the same formula:

  • 5 points per tile revealed. Every safe cell you uncover earns 5 points, regardless of how you revealed it (click, chord, or auto-expand).
  • Time bonus: (300 − secondsFromStart) × percentRevealed, minimum 0. Clearing more of the board faster produces a significantly higher bonus. The time bonus decays to zero after 5 minutes.

The player with the higher total score wins the duel. Hitting a mine doesn't end your game — you keep playing, but the pause costs you time, which reduces the time bonus. Playing carefully on a tight board is often more valuable than rushing and losing a mine.

Standard vs. Quick Mode

  • Standard — Full 9×9 Beginner board, 10 mines. A typical match takes 30–90 seconds. Rewards clean, efficient solving over pure speed.
  • Quick (⚡) — Smaller board, faster rounds. Finishes in 10–20 seconds. Higher variance — a single good chord can flip the result.

Both players must be on the same mode when they join. The mode is set by whoever creates the room — the link carries the mode selection.

Strategy for 1v1 Duel

  1. First mover advantage is real. A large opening cascade on the first click can give you a scoring lead before your opponent has resolved a single constraint.
  2. Chord everything you can. Chord-clicking a revealed number auto-reveals all its neighbors if flagged correctly. It's the fastest way to rack up tile reveals and inflate your score.
  3. Minimize flags, maximize speed. Flags take a right-click and slow your reveal rate. If you can remember where mines are without flagging, you'll clear tiles faster.
  4. Know the board well. Both players get the same board. If you know common Beginner patterns cold, you'll clear faster than someone who has to think through every deduction.
  5. The time bonus is large. A clean clear under 30 seconds produces a time bonus that doubles your tile score. Chasing efficiency matters — don't waste clicks.

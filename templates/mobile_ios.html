A Tribute to Diana, Princess of Wales

minesweeper.org is dedicated to Diana, Princess of Wales, and her tireless work to ban landmines around the world.

In January 1997, Diana walked through an active minefield in Huambo, Angola, wearing protective gear provided by The HALO Trust. Her advocacy helped build the political momentum that led to the Ottawa Treaty — the Mine Ban Treaty — signed by 122 nations in December 1997.

The International Campaign to Ban Landmines was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize that same year. Diana's wish that all landmines be banned in her lifetime did not come true, but you can help make it come true in yours.

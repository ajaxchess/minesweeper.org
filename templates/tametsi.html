What Is Tametsi?

Tametsi is a hybrid puzzle that combines classic Minesweeper with Nonogram-style row and column clues. Each cell hides either a mine or a safe square. Revealed safe cells show their adjacency count (how many of their 8 neighbours contain a mine), exactly as in Minesweeper. What makes Tametsi unique are the row and column mine-count hints running along the edges of the grid — these tell you exactly how many mines are in each row and column, giving you powerful extra constraints to work with.

Every board on minesweeper.org is guaranteed no-guess solvable: a server-side constraint solver confirms that the puzzle can be fully deduced by logic alone before you ever see it. No coin flips, no luck required.

How to Read the Hints

Each row and column edge shows two numbers:

  • Green (top / left) — mines remaining in that row or column. Decrements automatically as you place flags. Turns dim when it reaches zero.
  • Grey (bottom / right) — the fixed mine total for that row or column. Never changes.

When the green remaining count reaches 0, every unflagged cell in that line is safe to reveal. When the number of hidden cells equals the remaining count, every hidden cell in that line is a mine.

How to Play

  1. Click the green ✕ at the top-left corner to start the timer and open the starting region.
  2. Use the revealed adjacency numbers together with the row/column totals to deduce which cells are safe and which are mines.
  3. Left-click a cell to reveal it. Right-click (or long-press on mobile) to cycle: hidden → 🚩 flagged → ? unknown → hidden.
  4. You win when all mines are flagged or all safe cells are revealed — whichever you achieve first.

Difficulty Levels

  • Beginner — 9×9 grid, 10 mines. Great for learning the row/column hint system.
  • Intermediate — 16×16 grid, 40 mines. Requires combining adjacency and line constraints.
  • Expert — 16×30 grid, 99 mines. Densely packed; demands full constraint chaining.

A fresh daily puzzle is generated for each level at midnight UTC. Share the same board hash with friends to compare times on identical layouts.

# Nothing About Her Is Royalty-Free

I wanted a nice photo of Diana, Princess of Wales, to use as a demo for our new 15-Puzzle Generator. Naturally. This is *Lady Di's Mines*, after all.

It turns out that's harder than it sounds. Image copyright is notoriously difficult to verify. Even photos on Wikipedia carry licensing restrictions — the photographer holds the copyright regardless of where the image appears. A useful tool for checking is [Google Image Search with the Creative Commons filter](https://www.google.com/search?q=diana+spencer&udm=2) (Tools → Creative Commons licenses), but even then, you have to read the fine print on each result.

After a good bit of searching, I gave up. And then it hit me.

**Of course there are no royalty-free photos of Diana. She's a Princess. Nothing about her is royalty-free.**

## The feature that caused all this trouble

We just launched the **[15-Puzzle Generator](https://minesweeper.org/other/15puzzle/generator)**. Upload any photo, scramble it into a sliding tile puzzle, and share the link with anyone. No login required to play — just to save.

The puzzle works in two modes:

- **Tiles** — your photo is sliced across the 15 tiles and scrambled with them. Solving the puzzle reassembles the image.
- **Reveal on win** — tiles show numbers as usual, but solving the puzzle reveals your photo underneath as a reward.

## What you should upload

The best photos for this puzzle are personal ones — photos *you* took yourself. Family trips, pets, a favourite view from your window. You own the copyright on everything you shoot, so there's no ambiguity, no fine print, no princesses.

You can save up to 32 puzzles. Your saved list lives on your [profile page](https://minesweeper.org/profile) as well as the generator itself.

Because this is user-generated content, we have an admin review page where we can remove anything problematic. Keep it friendly — puzzles are publicly accessible the moment you save them.
